\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper has presented a controlled comparison of Baseline HPA (CPU)
and KEDA (queueLength) for a RabbitMQ-backed Spring Boot consumer
running on a single-node K3s cluster. With all non-signal parameters
held equal over $n\,=\,3$ trials per group, KEDA reacted $10.4$\,s
faster, drained the backlog $12.2$\,s sooner, and was the only scaler
to release capacity during the trial; the Baseline HPA's CPU signal
remained pinned at the pod's \texttt{limits.cpu} throughout the drain
and therefore could not trigger a downscale until after the backlog
was gone. For queue-backed microservices, scaling on the business
metric is not a preference but a measurable quality-of-service
improvement---independent of how aggressively the HPA
\texttt{behavior} block is tuned.
